The Problem

The Scaling Founder Crisis

Revenue scales. The backend doesn't. So you absorb it all — more decisions, more coordination, more fires, more dependency. Growth was supposed to create freedom. Instead it created a more expensive trap.

Don't know HOW

You can describe it. You can't architect it.

"I want my CRM to track clients through delivery." Clear ask. No path from there to a working pipeline.

Don't have WHO

Your team is great. Nobody on it thinks in systems.

A VA follows instructions. A great practitioner runs the work. Neither of them designs infrastructure.

Don't have WHEN

Infrastructure work keeps losing to the day-to-day.

You're too busy running operations to fix operations. Every weekend build dies on Monday.

That's why the weekend build fails, the new hire doesn't help, and the new tool doesn't stick.

How to work with us

Two ways forward.

The consultancy runs the build for you. The courses teach you to run it yourself. Same playbook, two delivery modes.

Done with you

Engagement — Four phases. One partnership.

Engagements move through four phases. Each one earns the next. By design we work with a small number of founders at a time — availability is limited and engagements are selective.

Phase 01

Audit · Ownership Discovery

Inventory of current systems. Identify risks and manual bottlenecks.

We get inside the operation — talk to you, cross-reference with your team, and spend time in the tools. You leave with a complete picture of where your infrastructure stands, what's exposed, and what's holding the business up.

Phase 02

Foundation · Structural Control

Stabilize and structure backend systems.

Before anything new gets built, we take what you already have and bring it under control. Tools get configured properly. Data flows where it should. The base is set so the next phase isn't built on quicksand.

Phase 03

Working Systems · Ownership in Motion

Restructure workflows and integrations for smooth execution.

This is where the real build happens. CRM architecture, workflow logic, cross-tool automations, permissions, documentation, and training. Work moves through the system without you pushing every step.

Phase 04

Continuity · Long-Term Stewardship

Ongoing accountability for backend operations.

After the systems are in motion, someone still needs to own that they keep working as the business changes. Phase 04 is the relationship that prevents decay — adjustments, improvements, and accountability for execution integrity over time.

What we own

Nine deliverables. One operational layer.

Together these form the operational layer that lets the business run without you pushing every step. Each one is owned end-to-end across the engagement, not handed back as a half-finished doc.

CRM structure

How your client data is organized — pipeline stages, custom fields, automations, views, permissions.

Workflow architecture

How work moves without you pushing it. Tasks, approvals, and handoffs route through defined paths.

Automation logic

Tools that talk to each other. CRM, invoicing, scheduling, and comms connected into one backend.

Permission layers

Who can do what. The team works inside the system without needing you to verify everything.

Process documentation

Living documentation owned and updated as the business evolves. Not a manual that decays.

Cross-tool integrations

One backend, not six islands. Update once, it reflects everywhere.

Training & enablement

The team learns to operate confidently within the new infrastructure — not just hand-them-a-doc.

Operational tracking

Dashboards and reporting that give you visibility without checking each tool or asking each person.

Execution integrity

When the business changes — new clients, new team, new tools — the infrastructure adapts. Someone is accountable.

Fit Check

Who this is for.

We work with three founders at a time. Slots are deliberately scarce because the depth is the value. Engagements rotate by design.

It's a fit if you're —

  • → A founder doing $1M–$10M in revenue
  • → Running a team of 5–25 people
  • → Built your business around a craft that isn't operations — practitioner, advisor, owner-operator
  • → Still the final escalation point for everything that breaks
  • → Comfortable with a $3K+ engagement and choosing one partner over stitching freelancers

It's not a fit if you're —

  • × Pre-revenue or solopreneur
  • × Running a coaching or info-product business
  • × A tech founder, fractional COO, or systems consultant — your problem is different
  • × Already have an ops or systems owner in seat
